# PMMS Separation-of-Duties Matrix

**Status:** Draft Complete — Pending Security, Domain, and Stakeholder Validation
**Related:** [phase-0.3-access-and-assignment-architecture.md](phase-0.3-access-and-assignment-architecture.md) · [role-catalog.md](role-catalog.md) · [permission-catalog.md](permission-catalog.md) · [high-integrity-access-controls.md](high-integrity-access-controls.md)

This matrix identifies role/action combinations where the same individual initiating and approving/certifying the same transaction creates unacceptable risk. **These are architecture controls proposed by this documentation package — they are not asserted as official DepEd policy.** Each entry requires validation by a designated owner before being enforced as a hard rule in implementation.

| SOD-01 | Registration and Eligibility | `athlete-registration.submit` / `eligibility-case.review` | `eligibility-case.approve` | High — self-approval of one's own submission or review undermines the entire eligibility-integrity chain (RSK-01) | Same individual must never hold both the submitting/reviewing assignment and the approving assignment for the *same case* | None identified — this should be a hard rule, not merely discouraged | Every eligibility decision | To be identified (Eligibility Authority per [Phase 0.1 OD-07](../00-product/open-decisions.md#od-07--eligibility-authority)) | Open — enforcement mechanism pending OD-07 |
| SOD-02 | Scoring | `score-record.submit` | `score-record.validate` | Critical — the entering official validating their own entry defeats the purpose of validation (RSK-03) | The validating assignment must always be held by a different individual than the entering assignment, enforced at the specific score-record level | None identified for any competitive result; a non-competitive administrative test/drill may be exempted with clear labeling | Every score validation | To be identified (Technical Delegate) | Open — should be enforced structurally, not by policy alone |
| SOD-03 | Result Certification and Protest Resolution | `official-result.certify` | `protest.resolve` (on a protest against a result the same individual certified) | Critical — an adjudicator cannot fairly review their own certification decision | A Technical Delegate/adjudicator must recuse from resolving a protest against a result they personally certified; a substitute adjudicator is required | None identified | Every protest tied to a self-certified result | To be identified (per [Phase 0.1 OD-09](../00-product/open-decisions.md#od-09--protest-and-appeal-authority)) | **Blocking** — OD-09 |
| SOD-03b | Tournament Management and Protest Authority | Sole `tournament.generate-draw` + `official-assignment.create` authority | `protest.resolve` for the same sport | High — a Tournament Manager who is also the sole protest authority for their own sport's competition decisions creates a closed loop with no independent check | Protest resolution authority (Technical Delegate) should be a role distinct from Tournament Manager, even where the same meet has limited staff | Small meets with genuinely limited staffing may require a documented, elevated-review exception — not a default | Ongoing, per protest | To be identified | Requires validation |
| SOD-04 | Medal Tally | `medal-tally.recalculate` (encoding) | `medal-tally.certify` | Critical — the most publicly visible high-integrity output must not be self-certified | Encoder and Certifier must be different individuals for the same tally snapshot | None identified | Every tally certification | To be identified (per [Phase 0.1 OD-12](../00-product/open-decisions.md#od-12--medal-tally-rules)) | **Blocking** — OD-12 |
| SOD-05 | Accreditation and Access Override | `accreditation-credential.issue` / `.revoke` | `access-scan.override` | High — an individual who can both issue/revoke credentials and unilaterally override a scan denial could circumvent the credential system entirely without an independent check | Accreditation Officer (issuance/revocation) and the role authorized for `access-scan.override` (Security Coordinator) should be distinct roles, even if held by different people who happen to be on the same committee | Emergency access per [phase-0.3, Section 31](phase-0.3-access-and-assignment-architecture.md#31-emergency-and-break-glass-access) follows its own elevated-audit exception process, not this restriction being silently bypassed | Every override event | To be identified (Security Committee lead) | Recommended |
| SOD-06 | Finance | `expense.record` / `budget-allocation.create` | `expense.review` / `budget-allocation.approve` | High — self-approved expenses are a classic fraud/error vector | Encoder (Finance staff) and Approver (Finance Coordinator/head) must be different individuals | None identified for meet-level committee finance | Every financial approval | To be identified (Finance Committee lead) | Recommended |
| SOD-07 | Platform/Security Administration | `platform.configure` / `security-event.review` (ROLE-01/ROLE-03) | Any business approval/certification action (eligibility, results, tally, accreditation) | High — platform/security administrators holding business-approval authority blurs the line between infrastructure trust and business-decision trust, and makes security review of business decisions non-independent | Platform Super Administrator and Security Administrator should not concurrently hold a business-approval role (Eligibility Approver, Result Certifier, Tally Certifier, Accreditation Officer) | None identified | At every platform-role grant | To be identified (DepEd Leadership) | Recommended |
| SOD-08 | User Administration and Audit Review | `user-account.manage` / `security-event.review` | `audit-event.view` / `audit-event.export` (as the *sole* reviewer) | High — a user administrator or security administrator who is also the only audit reviewer can suppress evidence of their own privileged actions | Audit Viewer should be a distinct role from Platform/Security/Support Administrator; at minimum, audit review should never rely on a single individual who also holds administrative write authority | A very small platform team may require a temporary combined role with compensating controls (e.g., mandatory external periodic audit) | Continuous — this is itself an audit-of-audit concern | To be identified (DepEd Leadership) | Recommended |
| SOD-09 | Medical Records and Public Information | `medical-encounter.view-sensitive` / `medical-report.export` | `announcement.publish` / `public-content.view` (as publisher) | Critical — a role that can view highly sensitive medical data and also control public communications creates unacceptable exposure risk for the platform's most sensitive data category | Medical Officer/Staff roles must never be combined with Public Information Publisher or Media Coordinator roles for the same individual | None identified | At every Medical role grant | To be identified (Medical Team lead, Data Privacy and Legal Stakeholders) | **Blocking** — [Phase 0.1 OD-15](../00-product/open-decisions.md#od-15--medical-data-handling) |
| SOD-10 | Device Administration and Security Review | `device-identity.register` / `.revoke` (ICT Coordinator) | `security-event.review` / `access-log.view` (as sole reviewer) | Medium–High — the person who provisions/revokes devices should not be the only one reviewing whether those devices behaved securely | ICT Coordinator and Security Coordinator should be distinct roles; device-provisioning logs should be reviewable by Security independent of ICT | Very small ICT teams may require a documented compensating control | Periodic device-credential review (see [access-review-and-revocation.md](access-review-and-revocation.md)) | To be identified (ICT Committee, Security Committee) | Recommended |
| SOD-11 | Support Impersonation and Business Approval | `impersonation-session.request` / active impersonation session | Any approval/certification action performed *while impersonating* | Critical — impersonation exists for support/troubleshooting, not to allow a support operator to approve business transactions as if they were the impersonated user | An active impersonation session must **never** be permitted to execute an approval/certification/publication action — this should be a hard technical block, not a policy reminder | None — no exception | Every impersonation session, without exception | To be identified (Security Administrator) | Recommended — see [phase-0.3-access-and-assignment-architecture.md, Section 32](phase-0.3-access-and-assignment-architecture.md#32-impersonation-and-support-access) |

## Enforcement Principle

Every conflict above should be enforced **structurally** (the system does not present or does not accept the conflicting combination) rather than relying solely on policy/training. Where structural enforcement is not yet feasible for a specific conflict (implementation-phase concern), the conflict must still be **detectable** through the audit trail (per [authorization-decision-model.md, Step 16](authorization-decision-model.md#2-decision-sequence)) so that a violation is discoverable during review, even if not prevented in the moment.
